MEDICAL MEDICATIONS REFERENCE

DIABETES MEDICATIONS
Metformin
- Dosage: 500-2550mg daily in divided doses
- Side Effects: Nausea, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort, lactic acidosis (rare)
- Interactions: Alcohol, iodinated contrast media, certain antibiotics
- Monitoring: Kidney function, vitamin B12 levels

Insulin
- Types: Rapid-acting, short-acting, intermediate-acting, long-acting
- Administration: Subcutaneous injection, insulin pump
- Side Effects: Hypoglycemia, weight gain, injection site reactions
- Monitoring: Blood glucose levels, HbA1c

HYPERTENSION MEDICATIONS
ACE Inhibitors (Lisinopril, Enalapril)
- Dosage: 5-40mg daily
- Side Effects: Dry cough, dizziness, hyperkalemia, angioedema
- Interactions: NSAIDs, potassium supplements, lithium
- Monitoring: Blood pressure, kidney function, potassium levels

Calcium Channel Blockers (Amlodipine, Diltiazem)
- Dosage: 2.5-10mg daily
- Side Effects: Edema, dizziness, flushing, constipation
- Interactions: Grapefruit juice, beta-blockers, digoxin
- Monitoring: Blood pressure, heart rate, liver function

ASTHMA MEDICATIONS
Inhaled Corticosteroids (Fluticasone, Budesonide)
- Dosage: 100-1000mcg daily
- Side Effects: Oral thrush, hoarseness, growth suppression in children
- Interactions: Minimal systemic interactions
- Monitoring: Lung function, growth in children

Short-acting Beta Agonists (Albuterol)
- Dosage: 2 puffs every 4-6 hours as needed
- Side Effects: Tremor, tachycardia, nervousness, headache
- Interactions: Beta-blockers, diuretics
- Monitoring: Heart rate, blood pressure, symptom control

DEPRESSION MEDICATIONS
SSRIs (Fluoxetine, Sertraline, Escitalopram)
- Dosage: 10-80mg daily
- Side Effects: Nausea, sexual dysfunction, insomnia, weight changes
- Interactions: MAOIs, NSAIDs, warfarin, other antidepressants
- Monitoring: Mood, suicidal thoughts, liver function

SNRIs (Venlafaxine, Duloxetine)
- Dosage: 37.5-225mg daily
- Side Effects: Nausea, dizziness, sweating, blood pressure changes
- Interactions: MAOIs, alcohol, other antidepressants
- Monitoring: Blood pressure, liver function, mood changes

GENERAL MEDICATION PRINCIPLES
- Start Low, Go Slow: Begin with lowest effective dose
- Monitor Regularly: Regular assessment of effectiveness and side effects
- Patient Education: Clear instructions on administration and monitoring
- Drug Interactions: Comprehensive review of all medications
- Adherence Support: Strategies to improve medication compliance
