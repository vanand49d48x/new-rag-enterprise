MEDICAL TREATMENTS AND THERAPIES

DIABETES TREATMENTS
- Insulin Therapy: Required for Type 1 diabetes, may be needed for Type 2
- Oral Medications: Metformin, Sulfonylureas, DPP-4 inhibitors, SGLT2 inhibitors
- Lifestyle Changes: Diet modification, regular exercise, weight management
- Blood Sugar Monitoring: Regular testing to maintain target levels
- Complications Management: Regular check-ups for heart, kidney, and eye health

HYPERTENSION TREATMENTS
- Lifestyle Modifications: Salt reduction, exercise, weight loss, stress management
- Medications: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, Calcium channel blockers, Diuretics
- Blood Pressure Monitoring: Regular home and clinical monitoring
- Dietary Changes: DASH diet, potassium-rich foods, limited alcohol
- Regular Medical Follow-up: Monthly to quarterly check-ups

ASTHMA TREATMENTS
- Controller Medications: Inhaled corticosteroids, long-acting beta agonists
- Rescue Medications: Short-acting beta agonists for acute symptoms
- Allergen Avoidance: Identifying and avoiding triggers
- Peak Flow Monitoring: Daily monitoring of lung function
- Action Plan: Personalized plan for symptom management

DEPRESSION TREATMENTS
- Psychotherapy: Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Interpersonal Therapy
- Medications: SSRIs, SNRIs, Tricyclic antidepressants, MAOIs
- Lifestyle Changes: Regular exercise, sleep hygiene, social support
- Alternative Therapies: Light therapy, meditation, acupuncture
- Crisis Management: Suicide prevention, emergency protocols

GENERAL TREATMENT PRINCIPLES
- Personalized Care: Treatment plans tailored to individual needs
- Multidisciplinary Approach: Team-based care involving multiple specialists
- Patient Education: Empowering patients with knowledge about their condition
- Regular Monitoring: Ongoing assessment of treatment effectiveness
- Prevention Focus: Addressing risk factors before complications develop
